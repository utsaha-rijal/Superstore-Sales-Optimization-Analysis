Superstore Sales & Profitability Analysis
An End-to-End Data Science Project focused on Business Intelligence and Predictive Analytics.

 Project Overview
This project analyzes a multi-year retail dataset to identify profitability leaks, segment customers based on behavioral data, and forecast future revenue. By transitioning from raw data to actionable insights, this analysis provides a roadmap for increasing net margins and optimizing marketing spend.

Key Deliverables
Profitability Audit: Identified "Loss-Making" sub-categories (Tables, Bookcases) and proposed a discount-cap strategy.

Customer Segmentation: Performed RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis to categorize 700+ customers into actionable segments (Champions, At-Risk, Loyalists).

Predictive Modeling: Built a Linear Regression model to forecast 2018 monthly sales, identifying a steady growth trend of ~$900/month.

Efficiency Analysis: Discovered that the Home Office segment is the most profit-efficient group (~14% margin), despite lower sales volume than the Consumer segment.

Tech Stack
Language: Python 3.x

Libraries: Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Scikit-Learn

Tools: Jupyter Notebook, Git

Project Structure
Data.csv: Raw transactional data (2014-2017).

Superstore_Analysis.ipynb: Complete Python code with professional documentation.

Images/: Exported visualizations (RFM Chart, Category Analysis, Sales Forecast).

 Key Insights & Recommendations
"Data is only as good as the decisions it enables."

Revenue vs. Profit: While the business saw a 51% increase in sales from 2014-2017, the profit-to-sales gap widened, suggesting a need for better operational efficiency.

Strategic Retention: Identified over 200 "At-Risk" customers. Re-engaging this group through targeted email campaigns is the #1 opportunity for immediate revenue recovery.

Inventory Planning: Proactive scaling of logistics is required for 2018 to meet the projected upward sales trajectory identified by the regression model.
